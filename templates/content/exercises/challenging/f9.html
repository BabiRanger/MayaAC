The output terminal of the two-port network in Figure 4(b) is connected to a load with impedance $Z_L = 4 - j2 \Omega$. Analyze the circuit to determine the current gain of the two-port network and the average power, $P$, delivered to the load.

Figure 4(b): Two-port network with source and load terminations

💡 Hint 1: Boundary Conditions (KVL)

Before using the Z-parameter matrix, establish the relationships at the ports. For Port 1: $\mathbf{V_1} = 10\angle 30^\circ - 10\mathbf{I_1}$. For Port 2, current $\mathbf{I_2}$ flows *into* the positive terminal, so $\mathbf{V_2} = -\mathbf{I_2}Z_L = (-4 + j2)\mathbf{I_2}$.

💡 Hint 2: Setting up the Matrix

Substitute your boundary conditions into the standard Z-parameter equations ($\mathbf{V_1} = Z_{11}\mathbf{I_1} + Z_{12}\mathbf{I_2}$ and $\mathbf{V_2} = Z_{21}\mathbf{I_1} + Z_{22}\mathbf{I_2}$). Group the $\mathbf{I_1}$ and $\mathbf{I_2}$ terms together to form a $2 \times 2$ matrix, which can then be solved using Cramer's Rule.

💡 Hint 3: Average Power Trap

Remember that average power ($P$) is only consumed by the *real* resistive part of the load, not the imaginary reactive part. Use the formula $P = \frac{1}{2}|\mathbf{I_2}|^2 R_L$, where $R_L = 4\Omega$.
