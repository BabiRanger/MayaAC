Question 2

Referring to the circuit shown in Figure 2, the supply sources are given as:

$v_s(t) = 15 \cos(5t + 20^\circ) \text{ V}$ and $i_s(t) = 20 \cos(5t) \text{ A}$

Calculate the phasor current $\mathbf{I_o}$ using the Mesh Analysis method.

Figure 2: Multi-mesh circuit with mixed AC sources

Your Solution:

💡 Hint 1: Frequency & Phasors

The angular frequency $\omega = 5 \text{ rad/s}$. Convert the sources into phasor form:
$\mathbf{V_s} = 15\angle20^\circ \text{ V}$ and $\mathbf{I_s} = 20\angle0^\circ \text{ A}$.

💡 Hint 2: Supermesh & Simplification

Combine the parallel $6\Omega$ and $j11\Omega$ branch to reduce the number of meshes. Since a current source is shared between Mesh 1 and Mesh 2, you must apply the Supermesh technique.

💡 Hint 3: Defining $I_o$

After solving for the mesh currents $\mathbf{I_2}$ and $\mathbf{I_3}$ using KVL and Cramer's rule, find the target current using the relationship: $\mathbf{I_o} = \mathbf{I_2} - \mathbf{I_3}$.

🔑 Show Answer

Answer: $\mathbf{I_o} = 4.85 \angle -138.21^\circ \text{ A}$
