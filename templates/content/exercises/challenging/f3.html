Question 1

Analyze the circuit shown in Figure 1(a) and use the Mesh analysis method to calculate all mesh currents.

Given: $v_s(t) = 60 \cos(t + 130^\circ) \text{ V}$ and $i_s(t) = 20 \cos(t) \text{ A}$

Figure 1(a): AC circuit with a dependent voltage source

Your Solution:

💡 Hint 1: Outer Mesh Current

Because the independent current source $I_s$ is on the outer branch of Mesh 1, you instantly know $\mathbf{I_1} = 20\angle 0^\circ \text{ A}$.

💡 Hint 2: Defining the Dependent Variable

Before writing your KVL equations, express the controlling voltage $V_x$ in terms of the mesh currents. $V_x$ is across the $j100\Omega$ inductor, so $\mathbf{V_x} = j100(\mathbf{I_3} - \mathbf{I_1})$.

💡 Hint 3: Substitution

Write your standard KVL equations for Mesh 2 and Mesh 3. Then, substitute your expression for $\mathbf{V_x}$ into both equations to get two equations with only two unknowns ($\mathbf{I_2}$ and $\mathbf{I_3}$).

🔑 Show Answer

$\mathbf{I_1}$ = $20 \angle 0^\circ \text{ A}$
$\mathbf{I_2}$ = $107.68 \angle 61.6^\circ \text{ A}$
$\mathbf{I_3}$ = $21.52 \angle 3.4^\circ \text{ A}$
