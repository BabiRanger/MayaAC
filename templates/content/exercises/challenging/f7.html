Question 2

Determine the magnitude, $H_{dB}$, and phase, $\phi$, of the following transfer function:

$$H(s) = \frac{5s^2 + 100s}{s^2 + 9s + 20}$$

Your Solution:

Write out your final expressions for $H_{dB}$ and $\phi$ as a function of $\omega$.

💡 Hint 1: Factorization

First, factor the numerator and denominator to expose the roots. The numerator shares a common factor of $5s$, and the denominator is a simple quadratic equation.

💡 Hint 2: Standard Bode Form

To easily extract magnitude and phase, convert the factors into the standard form $(1 + \frac{s}{a})$. You will need to divide the terms by their constants ($20$, $4$, and $5$).

💡 Hint 3: $j\omega$ Conversion

Substitute $s \rightarrow j\omega$. Remember that a lone $j\omega$ in the numerator contributes $+90^\circ$ to the phase, and terms in the denominator will have a negative sign in both the $20\log$ magnitude equation and the $\tan^{-1}$ phase equation.

🔑 Show Answer
  • Magnitude ($H_{dB}$): $20\log 5 + 20\log(\omega) + 20\log|1 + \frac{j\omega}{20}| - 20\log|1 + \frac{j\omega}{4}| - 20\log|1 + \frac{j\omega}{5}|$
  • Phase ($\phi$): $90^\circ + \tan^{-1}(\frac{\omega}{20}) - \tan^{-1}(\frac{\omega}{4}) - \tan^{-1}(\frac{\omega}{5})$
