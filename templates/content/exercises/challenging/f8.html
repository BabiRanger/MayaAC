Figure 4(a) shows a two-port network equivalent circuit. Determine the impedance ($z$) and hybrid ($h$) parameters for the circuit.

Figure 4(a): Two-port resistive network

Your Solution:

Enter your matrix values for Z and h parameters.

💡 Hint 1: Finding $Z_{11}$ and $Z_{21}$

To find the first column of the Z-matrix, open-circuit Port 2 ($I_2 = 0$). The total input impedance is $10\Omega$ in series with the parallel combination of $20\Omega$ and $(40+20)\Omega$. Use a current divider to find $Z_{21}$.

💡 Hint 2: Finding $h_{11}$ and $h_{21}$

Hybrid parameters are mixed. To find the first column, you must short-circuit Port 2 ($V_2 = 0$). By doing this, the rightmost $20\Omega$ resistor is bypassed entirely, leaving $10\Omega$ in series with $(40\Omega || 20\Omega)$.

💡 Hint 3: Finding $h_{22}$

To find $h_{22}$, open-circuit Port 1 ($I_1 = 0$). The parameter $h_{22}$ is an admittance (Siemens), calculated as $\frac{I_2}{V_2}$. It is the reciprocal of the equivalent impedance looking into Port 2, which is $((40+20)\Omega || 20\Omega)$.
