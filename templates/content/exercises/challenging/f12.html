Question 1

The two loads in the circuit shown in Figure 2(a) are as follows: Load 1 absorbs 8 kW at a leading power factor of 0.8, while Load 2 absorbs 20 kVA at a lagging power factor of 0.6. Given that the supply frequency is 50 Hz, determine:

  1. The total complex power and the overall power factor of the loads.
  2. The apparent power required to supply the loads.
  3. The magnitude of the source current $I_s$ and the average power loss in the line.
  4. The values of the impedance elements ($R, L, \text{or } C$) for each load.

Figure 2(a): AC power circuit with line impedance and multiple loads

Your Solution:

Enter your calculated values for total power, $I_s$, $P_{loss}$, and the component values.

💡 Hint 1: Leading vs. Lagging Signs

When calculating complex power ($S = P \pm jQ$), remember that a leading power factor means the reactive power ($Q$) is negative (capacitive). A lagging power factor means $Q$ is positive (inductive). Convert both loads into rectangular form ($P + jQ$) before adding them together.

💡 Hint 2: Line Loss Calculation

The average power loss in the transmission line only occurs across the real resistive part of the line impedance ($0.05\Omega$). Use the magnitude of the total current you found to calculate $P_{loss} = |I_s|^2 R_{line}$.

💡 Hint 3: Extracting R, L, and C

To find the impedance of each load, use $Z = \frac{V_L}{I}$. To find the individual load currents, use the complex power formula $S = V_L I^* \implies I = (\frac{S}{V_L})^*$. Once you have $Z = R + jX$, use $X_L = 2\pi f L$ for positive reactance and $X_C = \frac{1}{2\pi f C}$ for negative reactance to find the final component values.
