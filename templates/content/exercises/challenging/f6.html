Question 2

Find the transfer functions for the voltage gain, current gain, transfer impedance, and transfer admittance from the circuit shown in Figure 3.

Figure 3: Circuit for s-domain transfer function analysis

Your Solution:

Express your answers in terms of 's'.

💡 Hint 1: Convert to the s-domain

Before doing any analysis, transform the components: an inductor becomes $sL$ ($2s$) and a capacitor becomes $\frac{1}{sC}$ ($\frac{2}{s}$).

💡 Hint 2: Find Equivalent Impedances

Find the impedance of the parallel section ($Z_2$). The middle branch is $(2 + \frac{2}{s})$. Combine this in parallel with the $2\Omega$ load resistor. Then, add the series source components ($2\Omega$ and $2s$) to find $Z_T$.

💡 Hint 3: Defining the Outputs

Use the Voltage Divider rule to find $V_o$ in terms of $V_s$. Note that $I_o$ is simply $\frac{V_o}{2\Omega}$, and $I_s$ is the total source current, which is $\frac{V_s}{Z_T}$.

🔑 Show Answer
  • Voltage Gain ($\frac{V_o}{V_s}$): $\frac{1}{2s+2}$
  • Current Gain ($\frac{I_o}{I_s}$): $\frac{s+1}{2s+1}$
  • Transfer Impedance ($\frac{V_o}{I_s}$): $\frac{2s+2}{2s+1}$
  • Transfer Admittance ($\frac{I_o}{V_s}$): $\frac{1}{4s+4}$
