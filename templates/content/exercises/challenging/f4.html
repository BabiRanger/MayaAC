Question 1

For the circuit shown in Figure 1(b), determine the node voltages $\mathbf{V_1}$ and $\mathbf{V_2}$ using Nodal Analysis.

Given: Independent Current Source = $10\angle 45^\circ \text{ A}$, Voltage Source = $20\angle 0^\circ \text{ V}$.

Figure 1(b): AC circuit requiring supernode analysis

Your Solution:

💡 Hint 1: The Constraint Equation

A supernode always gives you a "free" equation based on the voltage source. Here, the positive terminal is at $V_1$ and negative at $V_2$, so: $\mathbf{V_1} - \mathbf{V_2} = 20\angle 0^\circ$.

💡 Hint 2: Supernode KCL

When writing KCL for the supernode, combine all currents leaving nodes 1 and 2. The equation should look like: $\frac{\mathbf{V_1}}{j4} + \frac{\mathbf{V_2}}{3} + \frac{\mathbf{V_2}}{-j1} = 10\angle 45^\circ$.

🔑 Show Answer

$\mathbf{V_1}$ = $31.91 \angle 16.15^\circ \text{ V}$
$\mathbf{V_2}$ = $13.91 \angle 39.83^\circ \text{ V}$
