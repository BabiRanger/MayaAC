Question 1

For the circuit shown in Figure 1, given $\omega = 1 \text{ rad/s}$:

  1. Calculate the total impedance, $\mathbf{Z_{total}}$ seen from terminal a-b.
  2. Calculate the current $\mathbf{I_x}$ if a current source $i_s(t) = 5 \cos(t + 20^\circ) \text{ A}$ is connected at terminal a-b.

Figure 1: AC Bridge-style Network with Inductors and Resistors

Your Solution:

💡 Hint 1: Reactance Conversion

Convert all inductors to phasors using $\mathbf{Z_L} = j\omega L$. Since $\omega = 1$, the values in Henrys (H) convert directly to $j\Omega$ (e.g., $15\text{H} \rightarrow j15\Omega$).

💡 Hint 2: Equivalent Impedance Steps

Simplify the network from right to left. Start by combining the $15\text{H}$ inductor in parallel with the $10\Omega$ resistor. Then, handle the series-parallel combination of the $4\text{H}$, $3\Omega$, and $6\text{H}$ components.

💡 Hint 3: Finding $I_x$

Once you have the total impedance of the right-hand branches ($\mathbf{Z_{eq3}}$), use the Current Divider Rule between the $2\text{H}$ inductor ($\mathbf{Z} = j2\Omega$) and $\mathbf{Z_{eq3}}$.

Total Impedance: $\mathbf{Z_{total}} = 0.26 + j1.76 \Omega$
Current: $\mathbf{I_x} = 4.45 \angle 11.6^\circ \text{ A}$
