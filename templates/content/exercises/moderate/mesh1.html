Question 1

For the circuit shown in Figure 8.34, find the branch current flowing through the central resistor $R_2$ ($6 \Omega$). Assume the loop currents $I_1$ and $I_2$ are flowing in the counter-clockwise directions as indicated.

Figure 8.34: Two-loop DC network with shared resistor and independent sources

Your Solution:

Enter the magnitude of the current through $R_2$ in Amperes.

💡 Hint 1: Shared Branch Resistor Polarities

Note that the polarities of the $6\ wastes \Omega$ resistor are different for each loop current. When writing KVL for Loop 1, the voltage across $R_2$ is expressed as $(I_1 - I2) \cdot R_2$. When writing KVL for Loop 2, it is expressed as $(I_2 - I_1) \cdot R_2$.

💡 Hint 2: Solving Loop Currents

Set up your two simultaneous KVL loop equations. Don't be alarmed if your calculated loop currents $I_1$ and $I_2$ come out as negative numbers—this simply means the actual physical currents are traveling clockwise opposite to the assumed diagram arrows.

🔑 Show Answer

Answer: $I_{R_2} = 1\text{ A}$
