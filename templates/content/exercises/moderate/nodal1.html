Question 1

For the circuit layout presented in Figure 8.51, apply nodal analysis to identify the node voltages, and then determine the final branch current $I_{R_2}$ passing through the central $4\ \Omega$ resistor.

Figure 8.51: DC network containing an independent current source parallel to a branch resistor

Your Solution:

Enter the final branch current magnitude flowing through $R_2$ in Amperes (up to two decimal places).

💡 Hint 1: Node Definitions

Define your primary essential nodes. Let $V_1$ be the voltage node sitting directly above the $64\text{ V}$ source branch connection and to the left of $R_2$. Let $V_2$ be the voltage node to the right of $R_2$ and directly above $R_3$. Set the bottom rail as your ground reference ($0\text{ V}$).

💡 Hint 2: Handling the 2 A Source

Remember that the $2\text{ A}$ source is leaving Node $V_1$ and directly entering Node $V_2$. When applying KCL, make sure its sign is positive ($+2$) when analyzing currents leaving $V_1$, and negative ($-2$) when analyzing currents leaving $V_2$.

🔑 Show Answer

Answer: $V_1 = 37.82\text{ V}$, $V_2 = 32.73\text{ V}$, and $I_{R_2} = 1.27\text{ A}$
