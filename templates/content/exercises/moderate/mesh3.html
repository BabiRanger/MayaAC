Question 3

Consider the circuit with a voltage-controlled voltage source (VCVS) shown in Figure 18.12. Formulate the mesh equations, identify the controlling voltage variable $\mathbf{V}_x$ in terms of the mesh currents, and determine the final simplified KVL expression for **Loop 2**.

Figure 18.12: AC mesh network containing a dependent voltage source controlled by branch voltage Vx

Your Solution:

What is the controlling voltage equation for $\mathbf{V}_x$ expressed in terms of the mesh currents $\mathbf{I}_1$, $\mathbf{I}_2$, and resistance $R_2$?

💡 Hint 1: Tracking the Polarities of Controlled Sources

Pay close attention to the designated terminal signs on the diamond-shaped dependent source. Loop current $\mathbf{I}_2$ enters the negative terminal of the source first when traveling in a clockwise loop direction. According to standard voltage drop conventions, this contributes a $+ \mu\mathbf{V}_x$ term to your Loop 2 KVL summation.

💡 Hint 2: Establishing the Dependency Constraint

The variable controlling the dependent source is $\mathbf{V}_x$. Look at the given polarity signs for $\mathbf{V}_x$ across resistor $R_2$ (positive at the top, negative at the bottom ground rail). Because the positive sign is at the top, the downward-flowing current $\mathbf{I}_1$ aligns with the definition, while upward-flowing current $\mathbf{I}_2$ opposes it. This gives the dependency equation: $\mathbf{V}_x = (\mathbf{I}_1 - \mathbf{I}_2)R_2$.

🔑 Show Answer

Answer:
• Controlling Variable Constraint: $\mathbf{V}_x = (\mathbf{I}_1 - \mathbf{I}_2)R_2$
• Loop 2 Equation: $R_2(\mathbf{I}_2 - \mathbf{I}_1) + \mu R_2(\mathbf{I}_1 - \mathbf{I}_2) - \mathbf{I}_2R_3 = 0$
