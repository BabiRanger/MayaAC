Question 2

Determine the voltage across the inductor for the AC network shown in Figure 18.23.

Figure 18.23: AC network with mixed independent sources for nodal analysis

Your Solution:

Enter the final inductor voltage in polar form (e.g., 9.95 angle 1.88 degrees V).

💡 Hint 1: Node Identification

Define your primary essential nodes. Let $\mathbf{V}_1$ be the node above the inductor ($X_L$) and $\mathbf{V}_2$ be the node above the capacitor ($X_C$). Notice that the voltage across the inductor is exactly $\mathbf{V}_1 - 0$, which is just $\mathbf{V}_1$.

💡 Hint 2: Admittances Simplify the Algebra

Because there are parallel branches connected to the nodes, converting the impedances ($\mathbf{Z}_1$, $\mathbf{Z}_2$, etc.) into admittances ($\mathbf{Y} = 1/\mathbf{Z}$) in milli-Siemens ($\text{mS}$) early in your calculation will make setting up the Cramer's rule determinants much easier.
