Question 3

An AC voltage source of $120\text{ V (RMS)}$ drives a series load containing a $60\ \Omega$ resistor and an inductor with $X_L = 60.319\ \Omega$. The resulting RMS current is $1.410\text{ A}$. Calculate the True Power ($P$), Reactive Power ($Q$), and Apparent Power ($S$).

Figure: Power components for a mixed resistive/reactive load

Your Solution:

Enter P, Q, and S with their correct units (W, VAR, VA).

💡 Hint 1: Real vs. Reactive

Remember that only resistors consume True Power ($P = I^2R$ in Watts), and only inductors/capacitors generate Reactive Power ($Q = I^2X_L$ in VAR).

💡 Hint 2: The Power Triangle

Apparent Power ($S$) is the vector sum of $P$ and $Q$. You can calculate it directly via $S = I^2Z$, $S = V \times I$, or by using the Pythagorean theorem: $S = \sqrt{P^2 + Q^2}$.

🔑 Show Answer

Answer: $P = 119.365\text{ W}$, $Q = 119.998\text{ VAR}$, $S = 169.256\text{ VA}$
