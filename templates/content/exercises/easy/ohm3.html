Question 3

An AC voltage source of $\mathbf{E} = 120 \angle 0^\circ \text{ V}$ at $60\text{ Hz}$ drives a series load consisting of a $60\ \Omega$ resistor and a $160\text{ mH}$ inductor. Calculate the total complex impedance $\mathbf{Z}_{total}$ and the resulting phasor current $\mathbf{I}$.

Figure: AC circuit with both resistance and inductive reactance

Your Solution:

Enter the total impedance and current in polar form.

💡 Hint 1: Rectangular Addition

Because the components are in series, you can simply add their rectangular impedances together: $\mathbf{Z}_{total} = R + jX_L$.

💡 Hint 2: Polar Conversion

To divide the voltage by the impedance, convert the total impedance into polar form first. Magnitude = $\sqrt{R^2 + X_L^2}$. Angle = $\arctan(\frac{X_L}{R})$.

🔑 Show Answer

Answer: $\mathbf{Z}_{total} = 85.078 \angle 45.152^\circ\ \Omega$, $\mathbf{I} = 1.410 \angle -45.152^\circ\text{ A}$
