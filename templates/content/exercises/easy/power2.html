Question 2

An AC voltage source of $120\text{ V (RMS)}$ at $60\text{ Hz}$ drives a purely inductive load of $L = 160\text{ mH}$, resulting in a reactance of $X_L = 60.319\ \Omega$ and an RMS current of $1.989\text{ A}$. Calculate the True Power ($P$), Reactive Power ($Q$), and Apparent Power ($S$) of the circuit.

Figure: Power components for a purely reactive load

Your Solution:

Enter P, Q, and S with their correct units (W, VAR, VA).

💡 Hint 1: True Power of an Inductor

Pure inductors and pure capacitors do not dissipate true power (heat). Think about what your $R$ value is in this circuit, and plug it into $P = I^2R$.

💡 Hint 2: Reactive and Apparent Power

Use $Q = I^2X_L$ to find the reactive power in Volt-Amperes Reactive ($\text{VAR}$). Because $P = 0$, your Apparent Power ($S = V \times I$) will be entirely composed of this reactive power.

🔑 Show Answer

Answer: $P = 0\text{ W}$, $Q = 238.73\text{ VAR}$, $S = 238.73\text{ VA}$
