Question 2

An AC voltage source of $\mathbf{E} = 120 \angle 0^\circ \text{ V}$ at $60\text{ Hz}$ drives a purely inductive load of $L = 160\text{ mH}$. Calculate the inductive reactance $X_L$ and the resulting phasor current $\mathbf{I}$ in polar form.

Figure: AC source driving a purely inductive load

Your Solution:

Enter the reactance in Ohms and the current in polar form.

💡 Hint 1: Inductive Reactance

First, calculate the opposition to current flow in Ohms using the formula $X_L = 2\pi fL$. Don't forget to convert $160\text{ mH}$ to Henrys ($0.160\text{ H}$).

💡 Hint 2: AC Ohm's Law and Phase Angle

An inductor's impedance is $\mathbf{Z}_L = X_L \angle 90^\circ$. Use $\mathbf{I} = \frac{\mathbf{E}}{\mathbf{Z}_L}$. When dividing polar numbers, subtract the bottom angle from the top angle.

🔑 Show Answer

Answer: $X_L = 60.319\ \Omega$, $\mathbf{I} = 1.989 \angle -90^\circ\text{ A}$
