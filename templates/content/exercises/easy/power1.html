Question 1

An AC voltage source of $120\text{ V (RMS)}$ drives a purely resistive load of $R = 60\ \Omega$, resulting in an RMS current of $2\text{ A}$. Calculate the True Power ($P$), Reactive Power ($Q$), and Apparent Power ($S$) of the circuit.

Figure: Power components for a purely resistive load

Your Solution:

Enter P, Q, and S with their correct units (W, VAR, VA).

💡 Hint 1: True Power Formula

True Power is the power dissipated by resistance. Use the formula $P = I^2R$ and label it in Watts ($\text{W}$).

💡 Hint 2: Reactive and Apparent Power

Since there are no inductors or capacitors, the reactance ($X$) is zero. Apparent power is simply the product of the RMS voltage and RMS current ($S = V \times I$), labeled in Volt-Amperes ($\text{VA}$).

🔑 Show Answer

Answer: $P = 240\text{ W}$, $Q = 0\text{ VAR}$, $S = 240\text{ VA}$
