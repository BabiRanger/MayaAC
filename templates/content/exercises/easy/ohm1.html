Question 1

An AC voltage source of $\mathbf{E} = 120 \angle 0^\circ \text{ V}$ drives a purely resistive load of $R = 60\ \Omega$. Calculate the resulting phasor current $\mathbf{I}$ in polar form.

Figure: AC source driving a purely resistive load

Your Solution:

💡 Hint 1: Resistive Impedance

A pure resistor has no imaginary or reactive component. Its impedance is simply $\mathbf{Z} = R \angle 0^\circ$.

💡 Hint 2: AC Ohm's Law

Use the formula $\mathbf{I} = \frac{\mathbf{E}}{\mathbf{Z}}$. Divide the magnitudes and subtract the denominator's phase angle from the numerator's phase angle.

🔑 Show Answer

Answer: $\mathbf{I} = 2 \angle 0^\circ \text{ A}$
