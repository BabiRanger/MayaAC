Phasors: Pure Resistor

In a purely resistive circuit, the voltage across and the current through the resistor are in phase.

  • Phase Angle: The angle difference $\phi$ is $0^\circ$.
  • Phasor Relation: Both vectors point in the same direction:
    $$ \mathbf{V}_R = I \angle \theta, \quad \mathbf{I} = I \angle \theta $$
  • Implication: There is no "lead" or "lag" in a resistor.
