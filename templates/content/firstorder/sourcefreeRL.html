3. The Source-Free RL Circuit

Similarly, when an inductor is disconnected from a source, it releases its stored magnetic energy through the resistor. Because inductors oppose changes in current, we analyze the current response instead of voltage.

  • The Current Response: The current through the inductor at any time $t$ is:
    $$ i(t) = I_0 e^{-t/\tau} $$
  • Time Constant ($\tau$): For an RL circuit, the time constant is directly proportional to inductance and inversely proportional to resistance:
    $$ \tau = \frac{L}{R_{eq}} $$
