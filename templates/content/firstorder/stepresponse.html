4. Universal Formula & Common Pitfalls

Whether you are dealing with an RC or RL circuit, discharging or charging, there is a universal step-response formula that works for finding any voltage or current variable $x(t)$:

$$ x(t) = x(\infty) + [x(0) - x(\infty)]e^{-t/\tau} $$
  • $x(0)$ is the initial value at $t = 0$.
  • $x(\infty)$ is the final steady-state value at $t \to \infty$.
⚠️

The Time Constant Trap

The most common exam mistake is flipping the RL time constant. Remember: $\tau$ for an inductor is $L/R$, not $R/L$! Also, always find the Thevenin Equivalent Resistance ($R_{eq}$) seen by the storage element when calculating $\tau$.
