The Impedance Triangle

The Impedance Triangle is a graphical representation used to calculate the magnitude and phase angle of a circuit.

  • Horizontal: Represents the resistive part ($R$).
  • Vertical: Represents the reactive part ($X_L$ or $X_C$).
  • Hypotenuse: The magnitude of the total impedance $|Z|$:
    $$ |Z| = \sqrt{R^2 + X^2} $$
