2. The Core Parameters: $\alpha$ and $\omega_0$

To determine how an RLC circuit will behave, we must calculate two critical frequencies. Comparing these two values is the key to solving the entire circuit.

  • Resonant Frequency ($\omega_0$): The natural frequency at which energy oscillates between the L and C. It is the same for both series and parallel circuits:
    $$ \omega_0 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{LC}} $$
  • Neper Frequency / Damping Factor ($\alpha$): Determines how quickly the circuit's energy dissipates through the resistor.
    For Series RLC: $$ \alpha = \frac{R}{2L} $$
    For Parallel RLC: $$ \alpha = \frac{1}{2RC} $$
