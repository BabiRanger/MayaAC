3. Classifying the Natural Response

By comparing the Damping Factor ($\alpha$) to the Resonant Frequency ($\omega_0$), we classify the circuit's natural response into one of three distinct categories:

  • Overdamped ($\alpha > \omega_0$): The resistance is high. The energy dissipates slowly without ever oscillating. The response is a sum of two decaying exponentials.
  • Critically Damped ($\alpha = \omega_0$): The precise threshold. The circuit decays to zero as fast as possible without oscillating.
  • Underdamped ($\alpha < \omega_0$): The resistance is low. The energy bounces back and forth between L and C, causing a fading sinusoidal oscillation (a "ringing" effect) before finally dying out.
