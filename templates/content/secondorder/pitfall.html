4. Common Pitfalls: Initial Conditions

Solving a second-order circuit requires you to find the initial conditions at $t = 0^+$. Because it is a second-order equation, you need two pieces of information: the initial value (like $v(0)$) and its initial derivative (like $dv(0)/dt$).

  • Always find $v_C(0^-)$ and $i_L(0^-)$ before the switch moves!
  • Use KVL, KCL, and the component equations ($i = C \frac{dv}{dt}$ and $v = L \frac{di}{dt}$) at the exact moment $t = 0^+$ to find the initial derivatives.
⚠️

The Continuity Rule

Never forget the golden rules of energy storage components! Voltage across a capacitor cannot change instantly: $v_C(0^-) = v_C(0^+)$. Current through an inductor cannot change instantly: $i_L(0^-) = i_L(0^+)$. However, the voltage across an inductor and the current through a capacitor CAN jump instantly when a switch is thrown!
