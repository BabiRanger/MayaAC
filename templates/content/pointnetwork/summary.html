Summary & Common Mistakes

Two-port networks follow strict mathematical rules. If your circuit contains only passive elements (like resistors, inductors, and capacitors), it is usually a Reciprocal Network.

  • Reciprocal Rule: $\mathbf{z}_{12} = \mathbf{z}_{21}$ and $\mathbf{y}_{12} = \mathbf{y}_{21}$.
  • Matrix Conversion: The Y-parameter matrix is simply the inverse of the Z-parameter matrix: $[Y] = [Z]^{-1}$.

MISTAKE ALERT

Students often forget the direction of $I_2$. Because $I_2$ is defined as entering the network, if you are calculating an output load, the current flowing into the load is actually $-I_2$.

Exam Shortcut

For a standard T-network (often shaped like a "T" with three resistors), $z_{11}$ is just the sum of the two resistors connected to port 1, and $z_{22}$ is the sum of the two resistors connected to port 2. The middle resistor shared by both is $z_{12}$ and $z_{21}$!
