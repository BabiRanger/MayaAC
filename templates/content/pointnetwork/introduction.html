Introduction to Two-Port Networks

A port is simply a pair of terminals through which current can enter or leave. A Two-Port Network is an electrical circuit or device with two separate ports: an input and an output.

  • The "Black Box": We often treat the middle of the circuit as a black box. We only care about what happens at the terminals.
  • The 4 Variables: The entire network is defined by four variables:
    • Port 1 (Input): $V_1$ and $I_1$
    • Port 2 (Output): $V_2$ and $I_2$
  • Standard Convention: Both currents ($I_1$ and $I_2$) are always assumed to be entering the upper positive terminals.
