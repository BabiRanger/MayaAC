Active Element

A Voltage Source is a typical active element. Let's see how the sign convention handles power delivery.

  • Voltage Polarity: Defined from (+) to (-) across the source.
  • Current Direction: The current $i$ exits the (+) terminal, which means it enters the (-) terminal.
  • The Rule: Since current enters the negative terminal, we use the negative sign:
    $$ p = -vi $$
  • Conclusion: Since $p < 0$, the voltage source delivers power to the circuit.
